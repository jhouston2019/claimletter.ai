Insurance Claim Types We Handle

What is a Health Insurance Denial?

A health insurance denial means your insurance company has refused to cover a medical treatment, procedure, or medication. These denials often require a formal appeal within 60-180 days depending on your state. Our AI analyzes your specific denial and generates a compelling appeal letter.

Common Insurance Denials Explained

  • Medical Necessity: Treatment deemed not medically necessary
  • Pre-existing Condition: Coverage denied due to existing health issues
  • Experimental Treatment: New or unproven procedures
  • Out-of-Network: Provider not in insurance network
  • Prior Authorization: Required approval not obtained
  • Policy Exclusions: Specific treatments not covered
